📈 Trend Analysis

Trend 1: Reasoning Depth Is Becoming the Primary Competitive Axis

Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google all made reasoning-related moves this week. Chain-of-thought, extended thinking, and o3 pricing cuts point to the same conclusion: in H2 2025, model differentiation will be primarily about reasoning depth and controllability, not parameter count. For developers, model selection will shift from "which model is smarter" to "which model's reasoning process is more auditable and predictable."

Trend 2: Embodied AI Deployment Is Forking — Cloud vs. Edge

DeepMind chose edge, OpenAI chose cloud API openness. These paths have fundamentally different business models: edge means hardware lock-in and higher integration barriers but better latency and privacy; cloud means faster iteration and broader ecosystem but network dependency. Expect clear market validation signals by 2026.

📖 Today's Blog Pick

The Bitter Lesson — Rich Sutton

Essay · Rich Sutton · 2019 · incompleteideas.net

Re-reading this 2019 essay hits differently this week. Sutton's central argument — that scale and compute beat human-engineered priors in the long run — is the intellectual foundation for everything happening in reasoning compute right now. If you're wondering why everyone is racing to add more test-time compute, this is the best historical grounding.

  • Researchers consistently try to hard-code domain knowledge into systems — and consistently lose to brute computation over time
  • The only methods that scale reliably are those that improve as compute increases: search and learning
  • This is not a dismissal of human insight — it's a clear-eyed view of what makes a general method

Suitable for: anyone building or researching AI · Reading time: ~5 min

Today's Signal: The compute arms race has shifted from training to inference. Frameworks that efficiently allocate test-time compute will become the next infrastructure layer.
